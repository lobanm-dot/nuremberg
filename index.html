Historical Context

After World War II, the Allied powers held the Nuremberg Trials in Germany to put high-ranking Nazi leaders on trial for their actions during the war and the Holocaust. These were not trials for ordinary citizens or soldiers, but for top officials who helped organize, direct, or support Nazi crimes.

The trials aimed to expose the truth, hold leaders accountable, and establish standards in international law. You have been appointed to the International Military Tribunal. You must review the evidence and render judgment.

The Three Goals of the Tribunal

Expose the Truth

Reveal what the Nazi regime did. Document the Holocaust and wartime atrocities for the historical record.

Hold Leaders Accountable

Show that powerful leaders can be judged. Reinforce that “just following orders” is not enough to avoid responsibility.

Establish International Law

Set a legal precedent that war crimes and crimes against humanity have severe consequences on a global scale.
